Impact plasma amplification of the ancient lunar dynamo

This project investigates how impact-generated plasmas could amplify the ancient lunar dynamo field. The viability of this impact amplification process necessitates an internally generated magnetic field, bolstering the case for an ancient lunar dynamo. This work also shows that basin-forming impacts amplifying a core dynamo-generated magnetic field are a new mechanism for magnetizing airless bodies also apply to Mercury and asteroids as well. Lastly, our results make testable predictions for the properties of the strongest magnetization in the South-Pole Aitken (SPA) basin that can be tested with human and robotic missions planned for the lunar south pole in the next decade.
